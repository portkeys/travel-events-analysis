How to read this heatmap

Reading the chart: Each row is an event type, each column is a day. A dark cell means more events that day; a light/white cell means zero. Look for vertical columns of activity (a day with multiple event types firing = a high-intent user session) and horizontal patterns (which event types are consistently active vs. sporadic).

What the data tells us:

  • Travel Searched (126 total across 21/36 days) is the strongest intent signal. These users actively looked for lodging. However, searches only happen on 21 out of 36 days -- meaning most days have zero search activity. This is low and suggests the widget isn't surfacing the search experience effectively to most visitors.
  • No Availability Viewed (36 total) means users searched but found nothing available. Each of these is a lost booking opportunity. Consider expanding inventory for popular dates/destinations or showing alternative properties when availability is empty.
  • Property Added to Cart (22) and Checkout Clicked (16) are the highest-value events. When these cluster on the same days as searches, it indicates a healthy session where users move through the full funnel. Days with searches but no cart adds suggest pricing, availability, or UX friction.
  • Embed Widget Clicked (28 total) is relatively low compared to widget views (60K+). The click-through rate is ~0.05%, well below industry benchmarks: travel display ads average 0.47% CTR, and even programmatic display averages 0.1%. Personalized CTAs convert 202% better than generic ones (HubSpot, 330K+ CTAs studied). Getting from 0.05% to the travel display average of 0.47% would mean ~280 clicks instead of 28 -- a 10x improvement from the same traffic. Prioritize A/B testing widget headline, CTA copy, and page placement.

Activation Strategy

Based on the data above, here are recommended strategies to convert anonymous visitors into registered users and drive deeper engagement.

Capture Repeat Anonymous Users

2,837 users returned multiple times without registering. Many are already known on sister sites (BikeReg, SkiReg). Give them a reason to share their email.

  • Price-drop alerts: "Get notified when prices drop for Aspen lodging" -- requires email. Show after a search.
  • 2nd-visit registration nudge: Detect returning visitors and show a prompt: "Save your search and get exclusive deals."

Destination-Targeted Lead Magnets

Searches concentrate on a few destinations (e.g., Aspen, CO, USA). Use proven demand to capture emails.

  • Destination guides: "Download our insider guide to skiing in Banff" -- gated behind email. Create guides for top-searched destinations.
  • Create dedicated landing pages for top-searched destinations
  • Run email campaigns with deals for these locations

Optimize the Widget CTR

Widget CTR is 0.05% vs. 0.47% travel industry average. Even small improvements at 60K+ views would have outsized impact.

  • A/B test widget headline, CTA copy, and page placement
  • Add social proof (e.g., "12 people searched Aspen today")

Cross-Site Identity Resolution

Most repeat anonymous users come from BikeReg and SkiReg where they may already be registered. Stitch identities to unlock email-addressable audiences.

  • Investigate RudderStack Profiles to merge anonymous_id with sister site registrations
  • Cross-reference AREG user databases (BikeReg, SkiReg, RunReg, TriReg) with travel widget impression data
